Virtue Ethics

  • Virtue ethics accounts for the moral life, moral education, moral wisdom, familty, and social relationships.
  • Virtue refers to ones highest potential.
  • Aristotle expressed that the path to true happiness is living a virtuous life.
  • Apple acted in the interest of their proprietary software and hardware systems to protect the integrity of their business and their customers in terms of security.
  • The FBI is a government organization, and therefor we cannot use a single person's virtues to decide policy and couse of action for the case.

Pros

  1. Allows for moral and social influences to make decisions.
  2. Morally good people do what is right.

Cons

  1. Unanimous agreement cannot exist in a scenario where both participants are battling over who is trying to do the “most right thing.”
  2. Virtue ethics concentrates on the individual rather than the action and its outcomes.
