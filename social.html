Social Contract Theory

Developed by the philosopher Thomas Hobbes

Based on the ideology that “rational” has been defined, and is understood by the majority, thus people who behave rationally understand that cooperation is essential; however it is only possible when people mutually agree to follow guidelines

Ok, what does it require?

It requires two implicit agreements among civilized societies:

The establishment of such set of moral rules to govern relations among citizens

A government capable of enforcing these rules is thus social contract

Did anyone else work on social contract theory?

John Rawls, created a theory of justice within the SCT with two principles:

(1) each person may have a fully adequate” number of rights and liberties as long as they are consistent with everyone else having the same rights and liberties. (2) Social and economic inequalities must be fair and equal opportunities to assume are for everyone; and they are to be the greatest benefit of the least-advantaged member of society.

How does this relate to FBI v. Apple?

Using the Social Contract Theory to determine the results of the Apple versus FBI case we must first determine the “rational” parties involved: Apple, Inc., the FBI, and the San Bernardino community

The moral decision is whether Apple should supply the technology that will allow the FBI access into Farook’s iPhone and forego the data privacy and access laws

Should Apple have helped the FBI or not?

Well...

Under the Social Contract Theory, if Farook, Apple Inc.’s customer, has a right to expect confidentiality, for which he does, then Apple, Inc. would have the duty to protect his information

However, Farook violated the moral rules that govern the community and did not abide by the rules, therefore it would conclude that Apple would have a moral obligation to assist the FBI in the investigation against Farook
